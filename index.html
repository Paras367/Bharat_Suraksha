Stay Safe from Digital Spam

Learn to identify scams, protect your data, and keep children safe.

🎣

Phishing (The Hook)

Phishing is a cybercrime where targets are contacted by email, telephone or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data.

Imagine a fisherman using fake food to catch a fish. Scammers send fake emails pretending to be your bank to steal your password!

📱

Smishing (SMS Scams)

Smishing uses SMS text messages to trick users into revealing personal information or downloading malware. Common in India via fake KYC or Lottery messages.

Be careful with text messages! If a stranger texts you saying you won a prize, don't click the link. Ask your parents first.

📞

Vishing (Voice Scams)

Voice phishing. Scammers call pretending to be police, bank officials, or tech support to demand money or OTPs.

Never tell anyone your secret codes (OTP) over the phone, even if they say they are from the police. Real police never ask for passwords.

🦠

Malware & Bad Apps

Malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access. Often hidden in cracked apps or unknown APK files.

Only download games and apps from the official Play Store. Don't install apps sent by strangers on WhatsApp.

🚨 Protection Against Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

We are committed to a safe internet for children. If you encounter illegal content involving minors, do not forward it. Report it immediately.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Never share personal photos with strangers.
  • Use privacy settings on social media.
  • Tell a trusted adult if someone makes you uncomfortable online.
📞 National Helpline: 1098 (Childline India)
